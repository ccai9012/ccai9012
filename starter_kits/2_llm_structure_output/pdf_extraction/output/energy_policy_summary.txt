### **Comparison and Summary of Energy Policies**  

#### **Common Energy Policy Goals**  
- **Reduce energy consumption** and costs across tribal buildings.  
- **Improve occupant comfort** and building safety.  
- **Guide future retrofit projects** through energy audits and data monitoring.  
- **Enhance community engagement** via outreach (presentations, flyers, videos).  
- **Secure funding/training** for long-term sustainability.  

#### **Most Comprehensive Plans**  
- **Aniak** and **Akiachak** stand out for detailed actions:  
  - Both include energy audits, maintenance checklists, training, and quarterly reporting.  
  - Akiachak explicitly seeks financing for retrofits, while Aniak emphasizes coordination with regional projects.  

#### **Unique or Innovative Actions**  
- **Atmautluak**: Interviews with experts to refine energy action plans.  
- **Kwigillingok**: Outreach via video, a less common method in other plans.  
- **Akiachak**: Monthly maintenance checklists (more frequent than others).  

#### **Main Differences in Stakeholders and Timelines**  
- **Stakeholders**:  
  - All involve tribes, CCHRC, Energy Audits of Alaska, and Nuvista Electric.  
  - **Atmautluak** uniquely includes "Alaskans" broadly; **Akiachak** specifies building occupants.  
- **Timelines**:  
  - Most focus on **2017â€“2018**, with quarterly reports.  
  - **Atmautluak** extends to **February 2019** for final review.  
  - **Akiachak** mentions ongoing maintenance beyond 2018.  

### **Key Takeaways**  
- Shared goals of efficiency, cost reduction, and comfort.  
- Aniak and Akiachak have the most structured plans.  
- Outreach and stakeholder engagement vary slightly by region.  
- Timelines are similar, but Atmautluak has a later review phase.
